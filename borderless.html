Remote Laboratory Platform

This is a web-based remote laboratory platform that enables anyone to perform experiments anytime, anywhere. All experiments are centralized in the Department of Applied Physics at PolyU and are qualified and maintained by our professors and university staff.

Through the PolyU server, remote experiment setups receive commands from users and send back responses. Users can monitor experiments via live cameras and retrieve sensor data in real time.

Benefits

  • Highly flexible adaptation for various learning and teaching modes, including class demonstrations, individual/group assignments, and systematic investigations.
  • Operable like traditional school laboratories, available anytime and anywhere with internet access.
  • Automated data acquisition supports students in collecting and analyzing large volumes of high-quality data, enabling them to draw well-reasoned conclusions.

Targeted at secondary school students, this platform provides a real-time remote laboratory setup designed to enhance STEM learning. I led a team of 4 undergraduate students for this project, designing and building prototypes for various STEM experiments using SolidWorks and 3D printing, and assisting in software development including Raspberry Pi and Arduino control systems as well as creating a live stream control interface.
